***Firebolt.*** Spell Attack: +7 to hit, reach 120 ft., one target. Hit: 16 (2d10+1d8) fire damage.

***Spells as Abilities.*** I don't think a stat block should ever require you to reference another book/document, so I've chosen to turn all spells into spell-like abilities, at least in presentation. I've included spell levels for all spells of 1st level or higher for the purposes of counterspelling.

A spellcaster will typically not need every one of their spell slots for a 3-6 round combat, so many have fewer here. They also won't need a bunch of RP spells prepared for a combat encounter, which is what stat blocks are for after all. Minute resource tracking is for players. The DM has too much going on to have to worry about that.

***Moments vs Tactics.*** Often, the optimal way to play a spellcaster is to spam their strongest spell. In that respect, many of these stat blocks are decidedly un-optimal, since I prioritize casting a greater variety of spells that will make for memorable moments that feel right for the classes. Typically, every time you repeat the same action it becomes less impactful and memorable, so generally I've represented a stat block's highest level spells as 1/day, even when they could do more.

 If the attack hits, the creature's movement speed is reduced to 0 until the end of the turn.

***Soul of Vengeance.*** As a reaction, the Paladin may make a Halberd attack against a creature under the effect of its Vow of Enmity when that creature makes an attack.

***Paladins are Complex.*** Something I discovered putting this project together is that of all the classes, Paladins are the most irreducible. Which is to say, on any given turn in combat they have the most viable options for how to use their Action, Bonus Action, and Reaction. I really didn't expect that, to be honest. Even more so, if you take into account Find Greater Steed.

***Find Greater Steed.*** A high level paladin should always be riding something cool. Give them whatever CR 2 creature fits thematically to ride, then have it act on their turn. If you were already going to be using a more powerful monster in the encounter, have them riding that instead and have them act on the same turn in initiative.
